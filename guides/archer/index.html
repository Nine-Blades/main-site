Scout Class Guide

Master the art of archery and reconnaissance in Amtgard

The Scout is Amtgard's archer and reconnaissance specialist. Scouts excel at ranged combat, tracking, and providing battlefield intelligence. With their unique combination of archery, stealth, and utility abilities, Scouts are versatile fighters who can adapt to many different combat situations.

Scouts are one of the few classes that can effectively engage enemies from a distance, making them valuable assets in both small skirmishes and large battles. Their tracking abilities also make them excellent hunters during quests and special scenarios.

This guide will help you understand the Scout's abilities, equipment, and strategies to become an effective archer and reconnaissance specialist in the Kingdom of the Nine Blades.

Class Stats

Sash: Green
Armor: Points: 3
Shields: Small
Weapons: Dagger, Short, Long, Heavy Thrown, Bow

Scout Abilities

Scouts have a variety of abilities focused on archery, tracking, and mobility. Here are some of the key abilities you'll have access to:

Tracking

Allows the Scout to track insubstantial players who may be moving through the area.

Usage:Target an Insubstantial Player within 20', announce "Tracking" x 3, the Insubstantial player immediately has their Insubstantial effect ended.

School: Sorcery Uses: 2/Life Charge x3 (ex) (Ambulant)

Dispel Magic

Allows the Scout to remove all the enchantments on a player. This is a powerful spell used to reduce an opponents capabilities.

Usage: Target a Player within 20', announce "By my power I dispel that magic” x 3, all Enchantments on target are removed.

School: Sorcery Uses: 1/Refresh Charge x5 (ex)

Stun Arrow

A special arrow that causes the target to be stunned for 30 seconds if hit in the limbs, or 60 seconds if hit in the torso.

Usage: Call "Stun Arrow" before firing.

Type: Offensive Uses: 1/life

Flame Arrow

A special arrow that does one point of damage to armor and continues through to do one point of damage to the target.

Usage: Call "Flame Arrow" before firing.

Type: Offensive Uses: 1/life

Phase Arrow

A special arrow that passes through all armor and shield to do one point of damage directly to the target.

Usage: Call "Phase Arrow" before firing.

Type: Offensive Uses: 1/game

Camouflage

Allows the Scout to become hidden from view when stationary and touching a natural feature.

Usage: Touch a tree, bush, or large rock and announce "Camouflage".

Type: Defensive Uses: 1/life

Playing a Scout Effectively

To be an effective Scout, you need to understand your role on the battlefield and how to maximize your unique abilities:

Combat Strategy

  • Maintain Distance: Your greatest advantage is range. Try to stay away from melee combat where other classes have the advantage.
  • Use Terrain: Position yourself on high ground or behind cover to make it harder for enemies to reach you.
  • Save Special Arrows: Don't use your special arrows (Stun, Flame, Phase) on easy targets. Save them for armored opponents or critical moments.
  • Support Your Team: Focus fire on enemies engaging your teammates, especially healers or other vulnerable allies.
  • Know When to Retreat: If enemies close in on you, don't hesitate to fall back to your team's line.

Reconnaissance

  • Scout Ahead: Use your Tracking and Camouflage abilities to gather information about enemy positions and movements.
  • Report Back: Information is only valuable if shared. Make sure to communicate what you've learned to your team.
  • Mark Targets: Call out high-value targets for your team to focus on.

Scout Equipment

As a Scout, your primary weapon will be a bow, but you should also carry backup weapons for close combat. Here's recommended equipment:

Bow

Your primary weapon. A well-constructed bow with good draw weight and accuracy is essential.

Arrows

Carry plenty of arrows (at least 5-10). Make sure they're well-padded and pass safety checks.

Backup Weapon

A short sword or dagger for when enemies get too close for archery.

Light Armor

Scouts can wear up to 3 points of armor. Leather or padded armor works well.

Scout Advancement Path

As you gain experience and levels in the Scout class, you'll gain access to more powerful abilities:

Level 1

  • Tracking
  • Bowyer (1/life)

Level 2

  • Stun Arrow (1/life)
  • Camouflage (1/life)

Level 3

  • Flame Arrow (1/life)
  • Improved Tracking (can track for longer periods)

Level 4

  • Phase Arrow (1/game)
  • Additional Stun Arrow (2/life total)

Level 5

  • Improved Camouflage (2/life)
  • Additional Flame Arrow (2/life total)

Level 6

  • Additional Phase Arrow (2/game total)
  • Master Archer (improved accuracy and range)
